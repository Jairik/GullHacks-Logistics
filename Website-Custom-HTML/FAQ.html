SU Hackathon FAQ

General Information

What is a hackathon, and why should I do it?
A hackathon is a weekend of fun and learning. You’ll become a better programmer, meet awesome people, and make cool projects. There will also be opportunities to win fun prizes and network with other programmers and our sponsors. Everybody of all skill levels is invited to attend!

Event Details

Is SU Hacks 2025 Free?
Yes! SU Hacks is completely free, and anybody who is eligible is welcome to join!
Will Hackers receive free swag and/or food?
Yes! Each hacker will receive a completely free T-shirt and stickers, along with Lunch, Dinner, and Snacks. Breakfast will not be provided, and more meal details will be announced closer to the event! If you have any food preferences/restrictions, feel free to disclose this on the form or contact us for next steps!
Who can register for SU Hacks?
TBD. You can find the registration form on the homepage if interested!
Is parking provided? Where do I go when I arrive?
All parking will be free for attendees in the designated lots! See the location page for more information regarding parking. More information is to be released soon about buildings.

Teams & Participation

How many people may we have in a team? Can I be a team of one?
Teams must consist of 2-4 people, with solo teams not being allowed (working on a team is an important skill!).
Do I need to have a team to attend?
Nope! While it is absolutely permissible to come in with a team, you can form your own and meet new people through our official discord server (see home page) or through the brief team-forming event at the beginning of the event!
How do I qualify for a track? Can I do more than one?
No need to register for a track! All tracks are automatically considered during judging, with teams being eligible for multiple categories.

Logistics & Support

How do I know what events are going on at what time?
The schedule page on this website shows a general overview of the event, with updates being announced in the discord.
What do I need to bring?
All you need is the device you plan to participate with (remember a charger!) and anything else you believe you may need. Wi-fi, sufficient outlets, and food will be provided!
Who do I contact for technical or coding questions?
Hackers are welcome to hop on the #code-help channel of the discord, where other members and mentors can suggest ideas!
Who can I contact if I have more questions or concerns?
Feel free to send an email to the official Hackathon email, HACKATHON@salisbury.edu. Additionally, marked organizers can be reached through the discord channel.
